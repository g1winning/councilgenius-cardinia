[CARDINIA SHIRE CONTEXT]
Cardinia Shire Council is a growth corridor council serving Melbourne's south-east fringe and peri-urban areas. Our municipality spans from the Dandenong foothills in the north (Emerald, Gembrook, Cockatoo) to coastal townships in the south (Koo Wee Rup, Tooradin). This context shapes service delivery and priorities.

Key context for responses:
- Geographic spread: Council serves communities across Pakenham, Officer, Beaconsfield, Emerald, Cockatoo, Gembrook, Koo Wee Rup, Lang Lang, Bunyip, and surrounding areas
- Rapid growth: Population forecast to grow from 118,000 to over 200,000 by 2041
- Diverse regions: Northern "hills" area (Dandenong foothills) and southern peri-urban plains
- Growth challenges: Infrastructure development, planning pressures, service capacity
- Heritage areas: Mix of farmland, environmental reserves, and emerging suburbs
- Regional diversity: Rural character in north and east, suburban growth in west and centre

[BIN COLLECTION DAYS & SERVICES]
Collection Schedule:
- General rubbish: Weekly collection (typically Tuesday, Wednesday or Thursday depending on area)
- Recycling: Fortnightly collection (alternating weeks with green waste where available)
- Food and Garden Waste (optional): Fortnightly collection (lime-green lid, paid service)

Bin Presentation Rules:
1. Place bins out the night before collection day
2. Bins must face toward the road with handles toward your property
3. Bins must not be overfilled - lid must close
4. Remove bins within 24 hours of collection
5. Do not place bins on verges earlier than the night before

Recycling Bin Guidelines:
- Recycling items must be placed LOOSE in the bin (NOT in bags)
- Rinse containers to remove food or liquids before placing
- Acceptable items: paper, cardboard, plastic bottles, glass, metal cans
- No plastic bags (they jam sorting machinery)
- No contaminants (food, liquids, hazardous materials)

Food and Garden Waste Bin:
- Lime-green lid identifies this service
- Collected every 2 weeks (alternate weeks from recycling)
- Optional paid service (separate annual fee)
- Accepts: food scraps, garden waste, organic materials, compostable liners
- Does NOT accept: plastic, meat bones, dairy in large quantities

What You CAN'T Recycle:
- Plastic bags (must go in general waste)
- Polystyrene/foam (must go in general waste)
- Asbestos or hazardous materials
- Medical waste or sharps
- Batteries (take to recycling depot)
- Paint or chemicals (hazardous waste facility)

Common Bin Problems:
If your bin wasn't emptied:
1. Report online at www.cardinia.vic.gov.au - fastest option
2. Call 1300 787 624 and provide your address
3. Check that bins are accessible and lids close properly
4. Council staff will investigate and respond within 5 business days

[HARD WASTE COLLECTION SERVICE]
Hard Waste Booking Information:
- Service: Free bookable collection service for large items
- Booking method: Phone 1300 787 624 and select the hard waste option
- Online booking: www.cardinia.vic.gov.au/hardwaste
- Free collections per year: 2 per household per calendar year
- Additional paid collections: Available (each household can book 2 extra paid collections)

Size Limits:
- Single booking: Maximum 2 cubic metres (2m x 1m x 1m)
- Double booking: Maximum 4 cubic metres (4m x 1m x 1m)

What Gets Collected and What Happens:
Recycled Items:
- E-waste (electronics) - Sent to e-waste recyclers
- Scrap metal - Recycled by metal recyclers
- Mattresses - Recycled/processed
- Tyres - Processed for reuse
- Green waste - Composted or mulched

Landfill Items:
- Furniture (sofas, chairs, tables)
- Toys and plastic items
- General household items
- Everything else (crushed and sent to landfill)

Presentation Guidelines:
1. Only put items out the weekend before your collection week
2. Do NOT put items out earlier
3. Divide items into 3 sections if possible:
   - Electrical and metals
   - Garden waste
   - General waste
4. Keep mattresses easily accessible (set aside separately)
5. Items must be safe for collection vehicle to handle
6. Do not mix hard waste with regular bins

Items NOT Accepted:
- Asbestos or building materials with asbestos
- Hazardous chemicals or paints
- Batteries or flammable materials
- Medical waste or sharps
- Soil or concrete (large quantities)

- Phone: 1300 787 624
- Online: www.cardinia.vic.gov.au/hardwaste
- Email: mail@cardinia.vic.gov.au

[PLANNING PERMITS]
Do I Need a Planning Permit?
Most building works, extensions, and land uses require a planning permit. Never start work without checking with Council first.

Ways to Check If You Need a Permit:
1. Check your property's zoning on VicPlan: www.planning.vic.gov.au/planning/vicplan
2. Contact Council planning department: 1300 787 624
3. Book a pre-application meeting (see below)
4. Ask a planning professional or architect

How to Apply for a Planning Permit:
1. Determine zoning and overlays affecting your property (use VicPlan)
2. Prepare application documents (plans, site information, etc.)
3. Submit via Council's ePlanning Portal (preferred) or in person at 20 Siding Avenue, Officer
4. Pay application fee
5. Council will assess and request further information if needed

Submission Options:
- Online ePlanning Portal: www.cardinia.vic.gov.au (fastest and preferred)

[PET REGISTRATION REQUIREMENTS]
Registration Deadline:
Pet registration must be renewed by 10 April each year. This is a legal requirement.

Who Must Register:
- All dogs over 3 months old
- All cats over 3 months old
- Registration required under Domestic Animals Act 1994

Microchipping Requirement:
All dogs and cats must be:
- Microchipped by a registered veterinarian
- Microchip details recorded with Council
- Required by Victorian State Government law

How to Register Your Pet:
1. Online registration (easiest and fastest method):
   - Visit www.cardinia.vic.gov.au
   - Complete online registration form
   - Pay fee online immediately
   - Registration tag posted within 5-10 working days
2. In person at Council customer service centre:
   - 20 Siding Avenue, Officer
   - Monday to Friday, 9:00am to 4:30pm
   - Bring proof of ownership and microchip details
3. By phone: 1300 787 624

Benefits of Registration:
- Lost animals quickly reunited with owners (via unique lifetime tag)
- Reduces number of roaming animals in community
- Supports safe and welcoming shared spaces (parks, playgrounds)
- Legal compliance
- Council support for animal welfare

What You Need to Register:
- Proof of ownership (purchase papers, vet records)
- Microchip number and details
- Vaccination records (if available)
- Description of your pet
- Your contact details

Registration Tag:
- Unique lifetime registration number
- Emailed to Council when registered
- Physical tag posted within 5-10 working days
- Cost varies - check current fees on website

Penalties for Non-Compliance:
- Fines: $395 per animal if not registered by 10 April
- Higher penalties if animals are impounded
- Does not exempt from other animal control laws

Renewing Registration:
- Renewal required every year by 10 April
- Reminders sent by Council (check mail)
- Can renew online, in person, or by phone
- Same process as initial registration

Change of Details:
If your contact details or pet information changes:
- Notify Council immediately
- Update details online or call 1300 787 624
- Ensure Council can contact you if pet is lost

Lost Dogs and Cats:
If your pet is lost:
1. Contact Council immediately: 1300 787 624
2. Report to local animal shelters and vets
3. Check Council's lost and found register
4. Post on community notice boards and social media
5. Registration tag helps reunite you with lost pets

Cat-Specific Information:
- All cats over 3 months must be registered
- Microchipping required
- Keep cats confined to your property where possible
- Be aware of impact on wildlife
- Collar and tag recommended in addition to microchip

Contact for Pet Services:
- Phone: 1300 787 624
- Email: mail@cardinia.vic.gov.au
- In person: 20 Siding Avenue, Officer
- Website: www.cardinia.vic.gov.au/info/20003/pets_and_animals/39/register_your_pet_or_renew_its_registration

SECTION 18: COMMON QUESTIONS & QUICK REFERENCE
================================================================================

[FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS]

Q: When is my bin collection day?
A: Bins are collected according to your address. Contact 1300 787 624 or check your rates notice, which typically shows collection day. Most areas have weekly general waste and fortnightly recycling on alternating weeks.

Q: How do I pay my rates?
A: Multiple options available: Flexipay (direct debit), BPAY, Post Billpay, Centrepay, cheque, or in person at 20 Siding Avenue, Officer. Pay online at www.cardinia.vic.gov.au or call 1300 787 624.

Q: When does my pet registration expire?
A: Pet registration must be renewed by 10 April each year. Register online at www.cardinia.vic.gov.au or call 1300 787 624. Fines of $395 per animal apply for non-compliance.

Q: Do I need a planning permit for my project?
A: Most building works require a planning permit. Check your property zoning at www.planning.vic.gov.au/vicplan or contact Council at 1300 787 624 for specific advice.

Q: Where is the nearest library?
A: Cardinia Shire has libraries at Pakenham (03 5940 6200) and Emerald (03 5949 4600), plus the Mobile Library. Visit www.myli.vic.gov.au for hours and services.

Q: How do I book a hard waste collection?
A: Call 1300 787 624 and select the hard waste option, or book online at www.cardinia.vic.gov.au/hardwaste. Two free collections per year are available per household.

Q: What time should I put my bins out?
A: Bins must be placed out the night before collection day. Remove them within 24 hours after collection.

Q: Can I park in the disability parking bay?
A: Only with a valid accessible parking permit. Apply at Council (1300 787 624) if you meet eligibility criteria.

Q: Who do I contact about a pothole or road damage?
A: Report via www.cardinia.vic.gov.au, call 1300 787 624, or email mail@cardinia.vic.gov.au. Council responds within 5 business days.

Q: Where can I find out about community grants?
A: Visit www.cardinia.vic.gov.au/info/20021/supporting_our_community/153/apply_for_a_grant for current grant programs and application information.

Q: What are the Council office hours?
A: Monday to Friday, 9:00am to 4:30pm at 20 Siding Avenue, Officer. Phone hours 8:30am to 5:00pm. Closed public holidays.

Q: Is there an accessible parking permit available?
A: Yes. Apply at Council with proof of eligibility. Phone 1300 787 624 for application details. Free for eligible residents.

Q: What should I do if my pet is lost?
A: Contact Council immediately (1300 787 624), local vets, and animal shelters. Register microchip details with Council so they can help reunite you if your pet is found.

Q: Can I apply for a rates payment plan?
A: Yes. Contact Council at 1300 787 624 before missing a payment. Flexible arrangements available for those experiencing hardship.

Q: How do I object to a planning application?
A: Submit objection via the online ePlanning portal, email mail@cardinia.vic.gov.au, in person at 20 Siding Avenue, or by post to PO Box 7, Pakenham.

Q: What recycling items need rinsing?
A: Any food or liquid residue should be rinsed from containers before placing in the recycling bin. Place items loose, not in bags.

SECTION 19: DETAILED SERVICE GUIDES & EXTENDED INFORMATION
================================================================================

[DETAILED WASTE & RECYCLING FAQ]

Q: Why can't I put recycling in bags?
A: Plastic bags tangle in the sorting machinery at the recycling facility, causing equipment jams and costly downtime. This slows processing for all residents. Always place items loose in the bin.

Q: What happens to items in my recycling bin?
A: Sorted materials are sent to specialized recyclers: paper/cardboard to paper mills, plastics to plastic processors, glass to glass manufacturers, and metals to metal recyclers. Some items cannot be recycled locally and may be exported.

Q: Can I put foam (polystyrene) in recycling?
A: No. Polystyrene foam (takeaway containers, packing peanuts) must go in general waste. While technically recyclable, most facilities don't process it due to volume and contamination issues.

Q: How often can I use the hard waste service?
A: Each household gets 2 free hard waste collections per calendar year. Additional paid collections available. Collections are separate events, not combined with regular bin collection.

Q: What if my hard waste exceeds the size limit?
A: Items must fit within the stated limits (2m x 1m x 1m for single booking, 4m x 1m x 1m for double). Oversized items may need to be split across multiple bookings or you can arrange a bulk waste service alternative.

Q: Are there any items I absolutely cannot put out for hard waste?
A: Yes - asbestos, hazardous chemicals, paint, medical waste, and sharps are prohibited. These require special disposal at hazardous waste facilities. Contact Council for guidance on proper disposal of these items.

Q: What's the difference between hard waste and bulk waste?
A: Hard waste is the free/paid bookable service for large items collected at your property. Bulk waste is an alternative service for larger volumes. Contact Council at 1300 787 624 for bulk waste options.

Q: Why was my bin not collected?
A: Common reasons: bin not accessible, lid doesn't close (overfilled), bin placed too early, bin blocked by parked car, or collection vehicle couldn't access the area due to obstacles. Check placement and contact Council if issue persists.

SOURCE_URL: www.cardinia.vic.gov.au/info/20002/rubbish_and_recycling

[DETAILED RATES & FINANCIAL FAQ]

Q: Why have my rates increased?
A: Rate increases can occur due to: Council-wide rate increases (set annually), property value changes affecting valuation, new improvements to property, or change in land use classification. Check your notice for breakdown.

Q: Can I dispute my property valuation?
A: Yes. The Victorian Valuer-General sets property values. If you believe your valuation is incorrect, you can request a review. Contact Council for the formal objection process and deadlines.

Q: What if I can't pay my rates?
A: Contact Council immediately before missing payments. Options available: payment plans (flexible arrangements), hardship applications, or other assistance. Council works with residents to find manageable solutions.

Q: Do rates include water and sewerage?
A: Typically yes, but it depends on your property type and connection status. Check your rates notice for specific charges. Some rural properties may not have water/sewerage connections.

Q: How is my property valued for rates?
A: The Victorian Valuer-General determines valuations based on comparable property sales, property characteristics, and location. Valuations are updated regularly. You can view your valuation details online.

Q: What are the payment penalties?
A: If rates are not paid by the due date, interest charges apply. Additional penalties and enforcement action (including legal costs) may apply for persistent non-payment. Pay promptly to avoid penalties.

Q: Can I pay rates by installment?
A: Yes. Standard practice is 4 instalments per year. Flexipay allows you to choose weekly, fortnightly, monthly, or quarterly payments. Sign up annually.

Q: Is there a discount for early payment?
A: Cardinia Shire Council does not typically offer early payment discounts, but Flexipay can help with budget management. Check your notice or contact Council for current incentives.

SOURCE_URL: www.cardinia.vic.gov.au/info/20062/pay_your_rates

[DETAILED PLANNING & BUILDING FAQ]

Q: What does "planning permit required" mean?
A: It means you must apply to and receive approval from Council before starting work. Starting work without a permit is illegal and may result in enforcement action.

Q: What's the difference between planning and building permits?
A: Planning permit: Approves the design, land use, and whether work is appropriate for the location. Building permit: Ensures construction meets safety and health standards. Many projects need both.

Q: Can I start work while my application is being assessed?
A: No. You must have an issued permit before starting any work. Starting without approval may result in enforcement action, fines, or requirement to undo work.

Q: How long does planning assessment take?
A: Typically 6-8 weeks, but can be longer if Council requests further information (RFI). Some complex applications need referrals to other agencies, extending timelines to 3-4 months.

Q: What if Council requests further information?
A: You have 14 days to provide information addressing Council's questions. If you don't respond, the application may lapse. Extension requests can be made if you need more time.

Q: What happens if I disagree with a planning decision?
A: You can request a review or appeal. Some decisions can be appealed to VCAT (Victorian Civil and Administrative Tribunal). Contact Council for appeal process and deadlines.

Q: Do I need a permit for fencing?
A: Usually no, but some situations require permits: heritage overlays, bushfire management overlays, or if height exceeds zone limits. Check with Council before building fences.

Q: What about extensions or renovations?
A: Most require permits. Pool fencing, deck structures, roof additions, and internal renovations may vary. Always check before starting - penalties for unpermitted work include stop work orders and fines.

Q: How do I find out what overlays affect my property?
A: Use VicPlan at www.planning.vic.gov.au/vicplan. Enter your address to see all overlays, zones, and restrictions. Council can also provide this information at 1300 787 624.

Q: What's a pre-application meeting?
A: Free or low-cost initial meeting with Council planners to discuss your proposal before formal application. Helps identify issues early and improves likelihood of approval.

SOURCE_URL: www.cardinia.vic.gov.au/info/93/planning

[DETAILED PET REGISTRATION FAQ]

Q: Why must my pet be microchipped?
A: Microchips are permanent identification that survive injury or loss of tags. If your lost pet is found and brought to a vet or shelter, they scan for the microchip and reunite you. Law requires all dogs and cats to be microchipped.

Q: Can I register my pet online?
A: Yes, online registration is the fastest method. Visit www.cardinia.vic.gov.au, complete the form, pay the fee online, and your pet's tag is posted within 5-10 working days.

Q: What's the registration deadline?
A: Pet registration must be renewed by 10 April each year. Reminders are sent by Council but you are responsible for renewing. Late registration incurs penalties.

Q: How much does pet registration cost?
A: Fees vary annually and by pet type. Check the Council website for current 2026 fees, or call 1300 787 624. Concession rates may apply for eligible residents.

Q: What if my pet has no microchip?
A: Contact a local veterinarian to arrange microchipping (usually $30-50). Get the microchip number, then register your pet with Council within the required timeframe.

Q: Can I transfer my pet's registration if I move?
A: If you move within Cardinia Shire, notify Council with your new address. If you move to another council area, you'll need to register with that council.

Q: What if I lose my pet's registration tag?
A: Contact Council immediately at 1300 787 624. You can request a replacement tag. Your microchip registration is permanent so your pet can still be identified.

Q: Are pensioners exempt from registration fees?
A: Check with Council about concession rates or exemptions for eligible pensioners. Call 1300 787 624 or check the website for current concession eligibility.

Q: What happens to unregistered pets if impounded?
A: Impounded unregistered pets may be rehomed or euthanized if not claimed. Registered pets with microchips are identified and owners contacted immediately. Registration saves lives.

SOURCE_URL: www.cardinia.vic.gov.au/info/20003/pets_and_animals

[BUILDING SITE REGULATIONS & SAFETY]

Q: What are building site work hours?
A: Typically Monday to Friday 7:00am to 6:00pm, Saturday 7:00am to 1:00pm, with no work on Sundays or public holidays. Hours may vary - check your permit conditions and the Community Local Law 2024.

Q: Do I need to notify neighbors about building works?
A: While not legally required, it's courteous and practical. Inform neighbors of expected duration, parking impacts, and noise schedules. Good relationships prevent disputes.

Q: What's the penalty for building site violations?
A: Violations of building site regulations (work outside hours, inadequate stormwater protection, safety issues) carry significant penalties. Building work may be halted until compliance achieved.

Q: What stormwater protection is required?
A: Building sites must prevent soil, waste, and materials entering stormwater drains. Sediment fences, erosion control, and covered waste areas required. Environmental breaches carry higher penalties.

Q: How long can a building permit be valid?
A: Most permits specify a lapsing date (often 2-4 years). If work hasn't commenced by this date, the permit expires and must be reapplied for. Request extensions if needed before lapsing.

Q: Can I extend my building permit?
A: Yes, contact Council before the permit lapses. Extensions are possible if work has commenced or if delays are justified. Extensions may incur additional fees.

SECTION 20: SPECIALIZED FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

[PROPERTY VALUATION & ASSESSMENT]

Q: When is my property valued?
A: The Victorian Valuer-General conducts general valuations periodically (usually annually) and specific valuations on request. Valuations can change based on property improvements, market conditions, or your request.

Q: How do I request a property valuation?
A: Contact the Victorian Valuer-General directly. Council can provide contact details or you can find them through the Department of Transport and Planning website.

Q: What factors affect my property value?
A: Location, property size, building condition, improvements, comparable sales, local amenities, zoning, overlays, and market conditions all affect valuation. Valuers consider all these factors.

Q: Can I appeal my valuation?
A: Yes. If you believe your valuation is incorrect, you can request a review or lodge a formal objection. Strict deadlines apply (usually 60 days). Contact the Valuer-General for appeal process.

Q: Does Council determine my property value?
A: No. The independent Victorian Valuer-General determines all property valuations. Council uses these values for rates assessment but does not set valuations.

[RATES ASSESSMENT DETAILS]

Q: Why is my rates bill higher than my neighbor's?
A: Rates are based on property valuation, not just size. Factors include: land value, location, zoning, building value, and amenities. Two similarly-sized properties can have very different values.

Q: Are rates different for owner-occupied vs. investment properties?
A: Rates may differ based on land use classification. Owner-occupied residential, rental properties, commercial, and rural may be assessed differently. Check your rates notice for classification.

Q: What discounts are available?
A: Specific hardship grants or deferrals may be available for eligible residents. Early payment doesn't typically receive discounts, but Flexipay provides budgeting flexibility. Call 1300 787 624 for eligibility.

Q: How often do rates increase?
A: Council sets rates annually within limits set by the State Government (Victorian Essential Services Commission). Increases typical 2-5% per annum but vary annually.

Q: Can rates increase mid-year?
A: Standard increase occurs each financial year. However, property-specific increases can occur during the year if your property is revalued or land use changes.

[BUILDING & RENOVATION DETAILS]

Q: Do I need a permit for a minor renovation?
A: Depends on scope. Internal cosmetic work (painting, wallpaper, carpet) doesn't need permits. Structural changes, additions, or significant alterations require permits. When in doubt, contact Council.

Q: What about adding a deck or pergola?
A: Generally requires a permit. Height, size, proximity to boundaries, and zoning restrictions apply. Some small pergolas exempt but always check first.

Q: Is a granny flat allowed?
A: Depends on property and zoning. Some areas allow secondary dwellings (granny flats) but specific requirements apply. Contact Council planning department for your property.

Q: What about solar panels?
A: Usually don't require planning permits. Building permit typically required for installation. Consult builder and Council for your specific situation.

Q: How much does a building permit cost?
A: Fees vary based on construction value (from $300 for small renovations to several thousand for major projects). Your building surveyor can provide fee estimate.

Q: What is an owner-builder?
A: You are the owner-builder if you own the property and undertake or supervise the building work yourself. You're responsible for obtaining permits and ensuring code compliance.

[WASTE SERVICE DETAILS]

Q: What if I miss the collection day?
A: Bins must be removed within 24 hours of collection. If you miss it, you'll need to wait for the next scheduled collection day. For urgent issues, contact Council.

Q: Can I request a different collection day?
A: Collection days are set by geographic zones. Changing days typically not available, but contact Council at 1300 787 624 if you have special circumstances.

Q: What if my bin is damaged?
A: Report damage to Council immediately. Replacement bins provided. Don't use damaged bins as they may not empty properly and create safety issues.

Q: Can I have more than one bin?
A: You can request additional bins or larger bin sizes. Contact Council for availability and charges. Some properties can have multiple bins.

Q: What about apartment or unit waste?
A: Check with your body corporate or building manager about waste arrangements. Some use communal collections; others have individual bins. Contact Council if unclear about your arrangements.

Q: Can I put yard waste in green waste bin?
A: Yes. Green/FOGO bins accept garden waste (grass clippings, leaves, branches under 50mm diameter). Larger branches go to hard waste service.

[PLANNING PERMIT PROCESS DETAILS]

Q: What is an RFI (Request for Further Information)?
A: Council asks for additional information/clarification about your application. You typically have 14 days to respond. Failure to respond may result in application lapsing.

Q: What if my application lapses?
A: Work hasn't started and Council didn't receive requested information. You must reapply and pay fees again. RFI deadlines strictly enforced.

Q: Can I appeal a planning decision?
A: Yes. You can request an internal review. Some decisions can be appealed to VCAT (Victorian Civil and Administrative Tribunal). Strict deadlines apply (10-20 days typically).

Q: What's a vegetation management plan?
A: For properties with environmental overlays or significant trees, a plan may be required showing how vegetation will be protected during building work.

Q: What is site hoarding and when required?
A: Temporary fencing around building sites. Typically required for sites on public land or where community safety is concern. Building permit conditions specify requirements.

Q: What happens at site inspections?
A: Council building inspector visits during construction to verify: footings, framing, services (electrical, plumbing), and final completion. Inspections required before building commences and at key stages.

[PET MANAGEMENT DETAILS]

Q: What vaccines does my pet need?
A: Check with your vet. Core vaccines recommended for all dogs (C3 combination) and cats (F3RCH combination). Annual boosters typically required. Vet advises on best schedule.

Q: What if my pet attacks someone?
A: You are liable for injuries and veterinary costs. Report all incidents to Council. Dangerous dog declarations may result in muzzling requirements or restrictions.

Q: Are there off-lead dog parks?
A: Check Council website for designated off-lead areas and dog parks. Rules and hours vary. Not all parks allow dogs off-lead.

Q: Can I keep multiple pets?
A: Yes, but registration required for each. No specific limit noted but consider if you can care for all appropriately. Contact Council about any specific concerns.

Q: What if my pet escapes?
A: Immediately contact Council (1300 787 624), local vets, animal shelters, and post online. Microchip greatly increases chance of reunion. Door-knock neighbors and post in local community groups.

Q: Are there breed restrictions?
A: Victorian legislation lists restricted dog breeds/types. Check restrictions on www.cardinia.vic.gov.au or contact Council for your specific breed.

[ROAD & INFRASTRUCTURE]

Q: Who maintains which roads?
A: Local roads: Council maintains. State highways: Department of Transport and Planning maintains. Private roads: Property owners/associations maintain. Contact Council if unsure which authority responsible.

Q: How do I report a pothole?
A: Online at www.cardinia.vic.gov.au (fastest), call 1300 787 624, or email mail@cardinia.vic.gov.au. Provide location, size, and any safety hazards.

Q: How long until my reported issue is fixed?
A: Council aims to respond within 5 business days. High-priority safety issues addressed faster. Keep your reference number and follow up if needed.

Q: Can I request a new footpath?
A: Council has programs for new footpath installation. Submit requests at www.cardinia.vic.gov.au or contact 1300 787 624. Not all requests can be accommodated immediately due to budget.

Q: Are there tree maintenance requests?
A: Report dangerous trees at www.cardinia.vic.gov.au. Council will assess and prioritize. Private property owners responsible for their own trees.

Q: When will roadworks be finished?
A: Check Council website for roadwork schedules and updates. Contact Project Management if timeline unclear. Contact Council main line for specific project information.

[LIBRARIES & CULTURAL SERVICES]

Q: What's the cost of library membership?
A: Free for Cardinia Shire residents. You need a library card to borrow items. Apply at any branch or online through Myli.

Q: Can I borrow items from other libraries?
A: Cardinia libraries are part of the Myli network. Through interlibrary loans, you can request items from other libraries (may take 1-2 weeks). Check with library for details.

Q: What borrowing periods apply?
A: Typically: Books 3 weeks, DVDs 1 week, CDs 1 week, magazines 1 week. Renewals available unless item is on hold. Check Myli website for current details.

Q: Are there library programs for kids?
A: Yes. Storytime sessions, school holiday programs, and educational activities. Check Myli website for schedules and registration information.

Q: Can I use the library wifi?
A: Yes. Free wifi available at library branches. Register with your library card. Useful for accessing online services and studying.

Q: What computers/printing available?
A: Library computers available (may require booking). Printing, photocopying, and scanning services available (small fees apply). Contact your local library for specifics.

[LEISURE & AQUATIC CENTERS]

Q: What pools operate in winter?
A: Cardinia Life (indoor) operates year-round. Outdoor pools (Emerald, Koo Wee Rup, Garfield, Pakenham) close March-November. Cardinia Life provides winter swimming/fitness options.

Q: What swimming lessons available?
A: Cardinia Life and other facilities offer swimming lessons for all ages. Infants through adults. Early booking recommended as lessons fill quickly.

Q: Are there disability access facilities?
A: Yes. Ramps, accessible change rooms, shower facilities, and pool accessibility features. Contact facility directly about specific needs.

Q: What programs beyond swimming offered?
A: Fitness classes, water aerobics, aqua therapy, gymnasium access, health coaching. Check Cardinia Life website for full program details and schedules.

Q: Can I get a casual pass?
A: Yes. Casual visits available. Daily, weekly, or multi-visit passes sold at facilities. Family passes often better value than individual tickets.

Q: What about spectator access?
A: Spectator areas available at some facilities. Call ahead to confirm viewing areas available and any policies about accompanying swimmers.

[COMMUNITY GRANTS & SUPPORT SERVICES]

Q: What types of projects get funded?
A: Grants support: wellbeing initiatives, facility improvements, community activities, environmental projects, capacity building. Each grant has specific eligible activities - check guidelines.

Q: Who can apply for grants?
A: Typically: Community organizations, not-for-profit groups, incorporated associations, volunteer-run groups. Individual applications rarely eligible. Your organization must be properly registered.

Q: What size grants available?
A: Ranges from $500 (small grants) to $35,000+ (capital works). Most programs have specific ranges. Larger grants often require matching funding or community contribution.

Q: How competitive are grants?
A: Highly competitive. Many applications for limited funding. Stronger applications: clear need, strong community benefit, good planning, realistic budgets, community support letters.

Q: When are deadlines?
A: Vary by program. Common deadlines: April (wellbeing grants), ongoing (capital works). Check Council website for all current open grants and deadlines.

Q: Do I need an ABN?
A: Not always required for initial application, but ABN required to receive funding if organization doesn't have one. Contact Council before applying if unsure.

[PROCESS TIMELINES - WHAT TO EXPECT]

PLANNING PERMIT ASSESSMENT:
Standard Process: 6-8 weeks
With RFI (Request for Further Information): 8-12+ weeks
With external referral: 10-15 weeks
- RFI Response deadline: 14 days
- Council assessment: 5-7 weeks
- Delegation/approval: 1 week

BUILDING PERMIT PROCESS:
Surveyor preparation: 2-4 weeks
Council assessment: 1-2 weeks (usually delegated to surveyor)
Inspections during work: Multiple (staged inspections)
Final certification: 1 week after completion

HARD WASTE COLLECTION:
Booking to collection: 1-2 weeks (depending on demand)
Collection window: Usually 1 week indicated
Presentation: Must put out weekend before collection week
Removal deadline: Within 24 hours after collection

PET REGISTRATION:
Online registration to tag receipt: 5-10 working days
In-person registration: Same day if paying at centre
Renewal by deadline: Must complete by 10 April annually
Microchipping: 1-3 days (done by vet)

RATES PAYMENT:
Payment to processing: Same day online, 2-3 days cheque
Hardship approval: 5-10 working days
Payment plan setup: 1-2 weeks
Overdue notice to enforcement: 30+ days

LIBRARY SERVICES:
New membership: Immediate (apply in-person or online)
Item borrowing: Available same day if in collection
Interlibrary loan: 1-2 weeks
Program booking: Varies (book in advance for popular programs)

COMMUNITY GRANT APPLICATION:
Application submission: Various deadlines (check Council website)
Assessment period: 4-8 weeks
Approval notification: 2-3 weeks after decision
Funding disbursement: Upon agreement signing (2-4 weeks)
